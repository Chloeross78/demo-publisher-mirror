Layering skincare is a great way to personalise your skincare regime. It’s important to go back to important scientific principles, making sure you apply products that act deeper in the skin - so typically, anti-ageing products, which are targeting the dermis, and water-based products are applied first and heavier creams are applied last.

Your Night-time Regime

You need to cleanse first, for obvious reason. The most important thing to realise is that when you’re taking off your makeup, the daily grime and excess oil, what you’re actually doing is clearing away for absorption of the next step, which is really important.

If you’re targeting particular problems in the skin, it’s likely that your next step is going to be a water-based serum, which is usually a pricey investment containing key ingredients like vitamin C, retinol and peptides. So, you want to apply this next to allow maximum absorption.

The next step is a moisturiser that you want to apply while your skin is still damp from cleansing. This way, you’ll maximise the amount of water that you lock in. It’s important to apply this after the serum otherwise your pricey investment will be in vain because it won’t get through.

Your Daytime Regime

Your daytime routine will be a little bit more complicated because there are more layers involved and we tend to wear makeup during the day. The same principles apply – cleanse to create the base for applying a serum. An antioxidant serum is a great addition for daytime to neutralise any free radicals that escape your sunscreen.

Then you moisturise where you need it and apply a sunscreen. Dr Bunting is evangelical about using a sunscreen all year round because it is such a key anti-ageing ingredient. The key is to find one that you’re comfortable with using every single day. This means finding one that has an elegant formulation, and when you do this, you’ll find that it can behave like a primer, creating a lovely base for makeup.

The key to layering is to give products time to dry so that you build a nice base for makeup and you’re not rushing. A worthwhile investment of your time to get the maximum out of your skincare.
