Pale is definitely interesting, but if you want to go bronze without the sun damage check out our Editor-in-Chief Susannah Taylor's favourite self tanners

Where to find more from the editorial team and our experts at Get The Gloss:
ONLINE: http://www.getthegloss.com
TWITTER: http://twitter.com/GetTheGloss
FACEBOOK: http://www.facebook.com/GetTheGlossUK
INSTAGRAM: http://instagram.com/getthegloss

DIY fake tan has come a long way from the streaky, stinky tanners of the 90’s, allowing you to risk it without smelling like a biscuit. In her latest Editor’s vlog, committed faker Susannah shares her top beauty recommendations for the perfect faux glow.

If you’re dipping a tentative toe in the water of fake tan, St.Tropez Gradual Tan In Shower Lotion, £9.50 (http://gtgloss.uk/1JviQXU) is for you. Hop in the shower, lather up and leave for 3 minutes before rinsing off. It’s fool-proof to apply and leaves you with a light, build-able glow-to-go.

Dove Summer Glow Nourishing Lotion, £2.62 (http://gtgloss.uk/1eAgbz2) is a beauty editor favourite, nourishing your skin whilst giving it a gentle tan. At under £3 it’s a complete steal, too.

For sun care and self tan fit for royalty, Susannah crows Clarins as Queen. This Self Tanning Instant Gel, £20 (http://gtgloss.uk/1Sgz2BP) has a fresh scent a non-oily texture. It sinks in quickly and develops into a lovely, smooth bronze without a hint of orange.

When it comes to your face, the last thing you want to tangle with is the risk of looking tangoed. Susannah’s all-time favourite is the gorgeous Sisley Self Tanning Hydrating Facial Skin Care, £75 (http://gtgloss.uk/1HjTFF2). Just remember to wipe it from your eyebrows with a cotton bud after application.

If you’re worried about unsightly tan-hands then this St Tropez self tan dry luxury oil, £25 (http://gtgloss.uk/1JRMqSZ) is ideal. It can be easily applied using the St Tropez Applicator Mitt (http://gtgloss.uk/1IqvlP9) and develops into a beautiful golden tan.

Chanel Les Beiges Healthy Glow Sheer Colour Stick, £32 (http://gtgloss.uk/1IqxQRt) is a blush that doubles as a highlighter. Make like a makeup artist and apply it on all the areas the sun would naturally hit, such as the cheekbones and bridge of the nose for a sun-kissed sheen.

For a faint glow sans shimmer, Susannah recommends By Terry Soleil Terrybly Hydra Bronzing Tinted Serum, £59 (http://gtgloss.uk/1KzCFff). This is all glow and no naff glitter.

For a bronzer that won’t break the bank, Susannah loves Bagsy Ray of Sunshine Perfect Glow Bronzing Powder, £12 (http://gtgloss.uk/1KyzO9e). The cute compact is the perfect size to slip into your handbag for touch ups on the go. For a natural look, lightly dust it all over your face with the Kiko Powder Make-up Brush: Face 103, £6.90 (http://gtgloss.uk/1MtUej4).
