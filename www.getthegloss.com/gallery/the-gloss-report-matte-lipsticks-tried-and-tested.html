The Gloss Report: matte lipsticks tried and tested

If you’re looking for a new lipstick, going matte could prove to be the perfect modern makeup update.

To see though if the days of chalky textured, drying mattes are truly behind us, we put 10 to the test to see how they delivered in terms of pigment, formulation and longevity. From MAC to Maybelline, Urban Decay to L’Oréal, click through our gallery to read our honest reviews of some of the biggest beauty brands in town to see what we really thought was worth spending your pennies on...


NEXT GALLERY: The year in beauty and health: 2015 >>>

2 / 11

MAC Matte Lipstick

Price £15.50

Buy online

Feedback “I’m a huge fan of a matte lip, having always preferred it to the sickly shimmer of a nineties ‘frosted’ look (why does bad glitter happen to good lipsticks?), but I often struggle with the texture. My constantly dry lips flake horribly under the chalky texture of most matte lipsticks, and adding a balm beforehand can create exactly the glossy look I’m trying to avoid.

“This little beauty from MAC was the silver, well, black bullet to my matte lippy woes. The texture is wonderfully creamy and hydrating, with nary a flake in sight. The colour payoff is brilliant, and I loved the pastel goth shade of Men Love Mystery. Purple lipstick sounds like a no-no, but this pinky lilac shade is perfectly wearable for those looking to dip their toes in a bold lip look. It’s not terribly long-wearing, but it doesn’t bleed or smudge either, and reapplication with a colour so fun is hardly a chore.”

Reviewer IB

Score 9/10

Shade Men Love Mystery


NEXT GALLERY: The year in beauty and health: 2015 >>>

3 / 11

Charlotte Tilbury Matte Revolution Lipstick

Price £23

Buy online

Feedback “This is probably the cashmere jumper of the lipstick world. It feels comforting to put on (it doesn’t drag as dryer mattes might), warms up both lips and the complexion and sits ‘just’ right thanks to an artfully sculpted bullet, just as a sumptuous sweater would.

“Get the right shade for your colouring, and your lips will indeed ‘glow’ as promised. Amazing Grace was bang on for me, and while initially a tad skeptical about the ‘lit from within’ lip thing, it’s true. A fusion of light reflective, 3D pigments somehow lifts the matte effect to make lips look full, plump, smooth and healthy, without the need for full on shine or a ‘kiss and run’ formula (the staying power is impressive). Flaking lips were formerly de rigueur where matte was concerned, but thankfully the labs seem to have skirted the issue with this one - I didn’t notice so much as a chap while wearing it. Quite the revolution, although the price is indeed ‘cashmere’ rather than cotton.”

Reviewer AH

Score 10/10

Shade: Amazing Grace

4 / 11

Estée Lauder Pure Envy Matte Sculpting Lipstick

Price £25

Buy online 

Feedback "As far as pigment goes, this lipstick delivered some serious punch. Velvet in texture and non-drying, it provided full even coverage for a statement lip that I’ve become a little bit addicted to. The shade I tried was called ‘Commanding’ and that it was - a deep and rich plum red that glided on with ease.

"It needed topping up after eating, but refreshingly the residue didn’t creep into my lip line and lip cracks like many other have done in the past. The bullet was wonderfully shaped too, with a thin tip to allow me to easily shape my pout and a flatter side to evenly smooth the colour on. Creamy, lightweight and eye-catching - I’ll definitely be using it again."   

Reviewer AM

Score 10/10

Shade: Commanding

5 / 11

Lipstick Queen Sinner Lipstick

Price £22

Buy online

Feedback “At 90% pure pigment, this matte isn’t messing around. If you were hoping for even the tiniest smidge of shimmer, sparkle or sheen, you won’t be finding it here, which will likely be quite the relief for most matte fanatics.

“Founder of Lipstick Queen Poppy Queen both knows what women want and doesn’t do things by halves, so Sinner will fulfill all of your full coverage, velvety lip dreams with minimal fallout thanks to a sturdy, stay put formula. Fire Red is a bright red orange that’s as flattering as it is bold on my pale skin, and both it’s capacity to keep lips looking even and alive (matte all too often = lifeless) and the fact that it doesn’t transfer easily onto cups, clothing or indeed, other people, are major plus points. The price not so much, but for quality and makeup that makes a statement all by itself, it’s worth it.”

Reviewer AH

Score 10/10

Shade: Fire Red

6 / 11

L’Oréal Color Riche Matte

Price £6.99

Buy online

Feedback “I’ve found cheaper mattes a bit on the chalky, crumbly side, but seeing as L’Oréal has been concocting Color Riche for 30 years, it’s no surprise that the French beauty house succeeds in making a pocket money matte mimic a more high end product.

"While it doesn’t quite possess the ‘supple’ feel of more expensive lipstick, the pigmentation is deep and lovely and it endures lunch and other lip activity very smoothly. It’s not quite as creamy as it claims, but it’s not drying either and this particular coral shade is classy rather than garish. It smells of melons to me, which makes it an all the more appropriate summer matte. Pick it up with your Prêt order and you’re very unlikely to harbour any regrets.”

Reviewer AH

Score 8/10

Shade: 228 VIP

7 / 11

Nars Semi Matte Lipstick

Price £20.00

Buy online 

Feedback “I can now say that I am now a total matte lipstick convert. Having spent years leaving Pat Butcher-esque lipstick marks on everything my lips touched within a 5 mile radius, I can truly say that ‘Transeurope Express’ has paved the way for more matte in my life - à bientôt sticky, gloopy lips.

“Even when applied lightly (since my local barista certainly doesn’t need that kind of shock in the morning), it had great staying power right up until after lunch and I was able to forget about it completely. I loved the colour as well – a great shade, especially as we move ever so swiftly into the autumn, and would work well day or night.”

Reviewer AEM

Score 9/10

Shade: Transeurope Express

8 / 11

Revlon ColorBurst Matte Balm

Price £7.99

Buy online

Feedback “My main worry about using a matte lip product was that it might be drying - if there’s no gloss, can there be hydration? Thankfully this Revlon crayon quashed all my doubts. This chunky twist-up matte crayon has an almost balmy feel, so while your lips feel soft the highly pigmented colour makes as bold a statement as you dare and lasts fairly well without feeling dry. The best part, though, is that it’s a crayon you can throw in your handbag and apply without even thinking about it - my kind of on-the-go beauty. I tried the new neutral ‘Enchanting’ shade - ideal for a natural, velvety matte nude lip.”

Reviewer JJ

Score 9/10

Shade: Enchanting

9 / 11

Topshop Lips

Price £8

Buy online 

Feedback “Being quite fair skinned, Topshop Lips in Watermelon was initially a bit of a jolt to the system as I glanced back at myself one overcast Tuesday morning. It’s the type of shade that would work seamlessly with a glorious summer tan or even just some sunkissed freckles. Basically, you don’t need a lot else going on – it’s perfect for that effortless look we all strive for on holiday. The old, ‘What, this? It’s just cheap and cheerful! (Net-a Porter).’ The colour lasted well, except that it started to gather slightly at the sides of my lips as time wore on so it didn’t maintain an even coating. However, the colour was fun and for £8, it’s a bargain ladies.”

Reviewer AEM

Score 7/10

Shade: Watermelon


NEXT GALLERY: The year in beauty and health: 2015 >>>

10 / 11

Maybelline Superstay 24hr DualEnded Lip Color

Price £8.99

Buy online

Feedback “This two-step lip colour has a spongey applicator one end (which if you’re ticklish like me is a bit of a challenge to use) for the colour and a twist-up clear lip balm on the other. The colour side glides on like a regular lip gloss, though is of course less fluid so it takes a few applications. You then wait two minutes for it to dry, which I thought would leave me with parched, cracked lips with caked-on colour but it dried fairly seamlessly. I can see, though, why it has the second step - my lips didn’t feel protected or moisturised at all after the colour, but I then applied the balm and it smoothed out the colour to a neat, matte finish while giving me that softer feel I needed. I’m not sure about 24 hours (who would wear it to bed?!) but while wearing it I drank multiple hot cups of tea and there was no transfer or smudging in the slightest. Once it’s on, it stays on.”

Reviewer JJ

Score 8/10

Shade: 845 Hot Brown

11 / 11

Urban Decay Matte Revolution Lipstick

Price £15.50

Buy online 

Feedback "I love a statement lip and as far as red lipsticks go, I’ve found my autumn/winter shade. With a creamy texture that left my lips feeling soft and supple and great colour payoff too, it’s a shade I’m certain to be found wearing again and again as the year goes by. I’m not really sure what else I can say?! It ticked all of the boxes for me when it came to pigment and longevity. Full marks."

Reviewer AM

Score 10/10

Shade: Matte Bad Blood
