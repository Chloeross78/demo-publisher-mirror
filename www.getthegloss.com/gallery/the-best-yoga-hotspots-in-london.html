The 12 best yoga hotspots in London

Whether you’re a vinyasa virgin or a yogini in the making, London is bubbling with yoga hotspots to help take your yoga practice to the next level.

With classes catering for every style and every skill level, it’s no wonder the discipline is now regarded as an essential part of many a person’s working week to help counteract the aches and pains caused by hunching over a computer on a daily basis. Which classes are really worth booking in for though? Here’s our pick of the best hotspots in town that we’d highly recommend you say “Namast-yay” to...

Follow Ayesha on Twitter and Instagram.

2 / 13

Another Space

Types of yoga: Vinyasa flow and hot yoga.

London location: Covent Garden.

Why hit this hotspot? With infra-red heating to create a dry, almost holiday-like warmth, white open spaces and beautiful studio-specific scents, this is hot yoga at its most luxurious. With patient, not pushy instructors (Sara is one of our personal faves) at hand to make sure proper form is always correctly observed, plus lovely changing facilities too, this is one hotspot certain to make you smile through the sweat.

Is an intro offer available? Yes - £20 for 2 credits plus a buddy pass.

www.anotherspace.london.


NEXT GALLERY: Glossy Posse Picks: Our summer holiday favourites >>>

3 / 13

Virgin Active

Type of yoga: Anti-gravity yoga.

London locations: Aldersgate, Bank, Broadgate, Crouch End, Kensington, Mayfair, Merchant Square, Notting Hill, Tower Bridge, Walbrook.

Why hit this hotspot? To take your yoga practice to new heights. Literally. A highlight on the club’s yoga menu, channel your inner Cirque du Soleil performer (the class’s inspiration) courtesy of strong yet silky hammocks that help even the most ungraceful feel their most elegant. Not only do they provide an extra degree of support for helping perfect trickier moves, but they also incorporate a creative take on inversions to help improve circulation. Plus, how does Savasana in a hammock sound? Pretty dreamy right...(we very nearly fell asleep in ours).

Is an intro offer available? Yes, check the website for current deals.

www.virginactive.co.uk.

4 / 13

Good Vibes Yoga

Type of yoga: Vinyasa flow, hot, restorative and yoga nidra.

London locations: Fitzrovia and Covent Garden.

Why hit this hotspot? A blissful hotspot that supplies a winning combination of expert tuition and invigorating classes to calm both body and mind, it’s no wonder Good Vibes has fast become one of our favourites. Softly lit, open and extremely calming, its variety of classes (including ones in its infra-red studio), helps boost flexibility and positivity in equal measure. Its restorative yoga class is worthy of particular praise in our opinion - simple but hugely effective, it helped knead out all of our pesky muscular niggles caused by a week sitting at a desk, leaving us walking noticeably taller and straighter the next day. Good vibes felt all round after this one.

Is an intro offer available? Yes - £45 for 30 days.

www.goodvibesfitness.co.uk.

5 / 13

Triyoga

Types of yoga: A range; from dynamic to the more restorative and meditation classes too.

London locations: Primrose Hill, Covent Garden, Soho, Chelsea, Camden.

Why hit this hotspot? Providing an all-encompassing approach, expect to find a whole host of far-reaching facilities to help give your yoga practice a multi-faceted edge. Alongside life workshops and the studios’ highly experienced therapists (including the incredible Dr Yogi - a massage with him will leave you walking on air), the classes provide a great range of styles and relaxation methods to choose from with each instructor offering their own unique twist to help visitors incorporate some much-needed restoration into their hectic working weeks. With white walls, stained glass windows and open spaces, it acts as a calming oasis amidst the chaos of the capital.

Is an intro offer available? Yes - 30 days for £40.

www.triyoga.co.uk.

6 / 13

Hotpod Yoga

Type of yoga: Hot vinyasa flow.

London locations: Notting Hill, Brixton, Hackney.

Why hit this hotspot? With classes taking place in what we can only describe as ‘cocoons of bliss,’ think of a visit to this literal hotspot as the perfect way to limber up rigid muscles and loosen up stiff limbs. Heated to 37 degrees, the pods are hot but not unbearably so and the effective combination of active and passive postures, tranquil purple lighting and chilled out playlist holds the key to not just a more mellow state of mind, but also a more flexible body too.

Is an intro offer available? Yes. Prices differ from location to location. See website for further details.

www.hotpodyoga.com.

7 / 13

Fat Buddha Yoga

Types of yoga: Dynamic vinyasa flow with elements of Ashtanga and Yin.

London locations: Holborn, Dalston, Shoreditch, Central London.

Why hit this hotspot? Founded by DJ and Nike yoga trainer Jessica Skye, expect each class at FBY to be as positive and cool as its creator. With her venues ranging from sunny rooftops to night club basements to five-star hotels, Jessica’s choice of residencies and tailor made playlists provide a variety and style that make her classes supremely distinct. Her choice of pop-ups are also second-to-none providing the best possible start or end to anyone’s day.

Drop-in classes are £10, events are £15 and 4 class passes are available for £38.

www.fatbuddhayoga.com.

8 / 13

barreASANA at barrecore

Type of yoga: A unique mixture of vinyasa flow and barre work.

London locations: Mayfair, Chelsea, Wimbledon, Kensington, Hampstead.

Why hit this hotspot? Designed to seamlessly blend the famous barrecore ‘barre body’ method with an effective ‘zen burn’, this fusion of the studio’s signature barrecoreMIXED class and yoga serves as an effective workout for both body and mind. Alternating bodyweight utilising interval training with yoga poses, it helps build length and tone as well as a more centred core and mind in one fell swoop. With a playlist that includes a more modern, but still incredibly relaxing range of hits from rock to reggae to hip hop, this class is definitely on the higher end of the price spectrum but is a must-try for barre fanatics and those looking to add an extra dimension to their yoga practice too.

Is an intro offer available? Yes - first class is priced at £20 and a one month unlimited pass is priced at £175.

www.barrecore.co.uk.

9 / 13

Fierce Grace

Types of yoga: Classes are based on classical Hatha yoga, Bikram and Ashtanga yoga.

London locations: Near Queen’s Park (West), Kentish Town (North), Old Street (City), Finchley, Primrose Hill, Brixton.

Why hit this hotspot? In particular, for their brilliant hot yoga classes. Its signature Fierce Grace class is guaranteed to make you break a sweat (without breaking a limb), thanks to great personal attention and modifications by the instructors to ensure best form and technique are mastered from the get-go. With plenty of space to perfect your three-legged Downward Dog without fear of kicking your fellow yogi square in the jar (not very zen...), the classes appeal to both beginners and experts alike.

Is an intro offer available? Yes - 30 consecutive days for £39.

www.fiercegrace.com.

10 / 13

Stretch

Type of yoga: Dynamic and restorative yoga.

London locations: Two studios based in East London.

Why hit this hotspot? There’s a real sense of community at this particular yoga studio - a fact evident from the feeling of encouragement and ease you feel from the first class. Pared back and personable, all pretention is left at the door to provide classes that encourage rather than intimidate to make stretching that much further all the more appealing. With great attention to detail paid by the team of expert instructors, it makes the world of yoga all the more simple to step into.

Classes start from £7.

www.stretch-london.com.

11 / 13

YogaBox

Type of yoga: Vinyasa flow open level.

London locations: Bermondsey Square Hotel and La Suite West.

Why hit this hotspot? Aptly called the ‘yin and yang of workouts,’ this unique fusion of boxing and yoga brings the benefits of HIIT and the restorative qualities of yoga into one class to provide a workout that definitely stands out in terms of creativity and intensity. Helping to improve balance and coordination on one hand and restoration and meditation on the other, founders Will Wheeler (behind its yogic element) and Chris Baugh (a professional boxer) have created something quite special for both engaging and releasing muscles, and flexing body and mind too.

Is an intro offer available? Yes - 2 for £20.

www.yogabox.london.


NEXT GALLERY: Glossy Posse Picks: Our summer holiday favourites >>>

12 / 13

Sangyé Yoga School

Type of yoga: Hatha yoga.

London locations: Ladbroke Grove.

Why hit this hotspot? With classes designed to provide a greater degree of transparency in terms of tradition and spirituality, expect close instruction, meditation and devotional chanting to feature in this yoga hotspot’s classes. Combined with a series of exercises to build suppleness and strength, it provides a vigorous workout both inside and out. Merging the physical with the philosophical in one workout, those interested in exploring the Indian system of yoga in an accessible way should definitely give this yoga school a go.

Is an intro offer available? Yes - £40 for 30 consecutive days of classes.

www.sangyeyoga.com.


NEXT GALLERY: Glossy Posse Picks: Our summer holiday favourites >>>

13 / 13

Rooted Yoga

Type of yoga: Restorative slow vinyasa flow.

London location: South Place Hotel, London.

Why hit this hotspot? For a noticeably personalised practice led by a brilliantly energising practitioner. Making your mat feel like a second home, founder Pandora Symes provides the perfect antidote to a heavy Saturday night before with her Sunday morning classes to help chase hangover woes away. Based at the beautiful five-star South Place Hotel, mats, towels and water are included and attendees are welcome to use the spa and its sauna and steam room afterwards too. Sounds like the perfect way to start a Sunday if you ask us.

Classes are £15 per session.

www.rootedlondon.com.
