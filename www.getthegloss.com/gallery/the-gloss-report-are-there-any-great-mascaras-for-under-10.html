The Gloss Report: Are there any great mascaras for under £10?

  • February 4th 2017
  • / Judy Johnson 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Bonnie Stowell of Spring Green London >>>

1 / 11

Budget Mascara Reviews

Do you have to spend big to get big lashes? That was the question we decided to investigate with this week's Gloss Report, as the GTG team put some of the best high street mascaras to the test to see if the budget brands are up to scratch. Trialling a whole range of brands that all sit neatly under the £10 price tag, we shared our honest reviews and rated them based on the promises they made and the results they gave. Take a look through our gallery to see which wands had the magic touch...


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Bonnie Stowell of Spring Green London >>>

2 / 11

3INA The Definition Mascara, £8.50

Buy online

The promise: “Totally waterproof mascara that won’t budge come rain or shine.”

We give it: 8/10

Review: "I have a bit of a turbulent relationship with mascara. I find that either it overstays its welcome long after I’ve tried to remove it, or it creeps slowly down my face before the day is out. I can’t win. I was pleasantly surprised by this relatively inexpensive 3INA Definition Mascara. It’s a brand that I hadn’t come across before (although I’ve since discovered they’ve got a shop in Covent Garden and are stocked on ASOS too). The packaging and general brand messaging is simple but cool, and they don’t have too many products in the collection (which I like). So on to the product itself. The wand is double sided, combining both comb and brush (nice touch) and it did stay on all day. It also didn’t have that grainy/flakey consistency that some cheaper mascaras have, where it leaves flecks under your eyes. I would describe it as “smooth” to apply. When it came to the time to take it off, I used an oil cleanser with a muslin cloth (Balance Me - amazing!) and it came off a treat. I don’t know whether that’s down to the cleanser itself or whether it’s just easy to take off anyway, but in any case I was pleased to avoid looking like a hungover student the next day."

Reviewer: AEM

3 / 11

Rimmel Scandaleyes Reloaded Mascara, £6.99

Buy online

The promise: “Leaves you with dangerously bold, lethally full lashes. Unstoppable volume for 24-hour impact.”

We give it: 6/10

Review: “I love a high street mascara, and some brands in particular are so renowned for their exceptional low-cost lash boosters that they’re the preferred option over luxe alternatives. Rimmel is normally one of them, but unfortunately Scandaleyes Reloaded didn’t quite live up to its many promises, although perhaps it was the overpromising that was partly the issue. A mascara that claims to withstand blood, sweat and tears for 24 hours yet sweeps off easily with a little warm water was always going to go one way or the other, and while it’s very easy to remove, I did end up with a few undereye splodges by midday. The smooth formula is remarkably clump free, but the large brush didn’t allow me to create impactful volume, although you will be able to layer the formula without any flaking. If you’re partial to a subtly defined look, it would be a good option for daytime, but if you’re after lashes that stop traffic, I’d opt for Rimmel’s Wonder’full Wake Me Up Mascara, £7.99 instead."

Reviewer: AH

4 / 11

Kiko Unmeasurable Length Mascara, £9.90

Buy online

The promise: A clump-free mascara that instantly lengthens

We give it: 7/10

Review: “I adore Kiko’s formulas and packaging so was keen to give this mascara a try. The wand is different to any I’ve used before; a plastic rather than bristle brush, it has five spiky spheres along its length, making it look rather weapon-like especially inside its metallic casing. Clumping was certainly not an issue, and it did give me a bit of oomph in the lengthening department; I like bold lashes so tried to build it up as much as possible, but found I reached a limit after a few coats (so the ‘unmeasurable’ length claim is not quite accurate). The spheres also make it quite tricky to coat the lower lash line without creating a mess, but otherwise it left my lashes looking longer and feathery, there was no flaking and it’s one I would use again.”

Reviewer: JJ

5 / 11

Sleek Makeup Lethal Length Mascara £6.99

Buy online

The promise: “Extend, curl and define your lashes in a single stroke. This formula provides long wearing, buildable texture that won’t dry.”

We give it: 8/10

Review: “Sleek Makeup is not a brand I have used before - their imagery is full of dramatic bases, statement brows and exaggerated liner flicks - not a good look when you are over 40 (check out Wendy Rowe’s anti-ageing makeup advice here). But statement lashes are something I can do with, so I was looking forward to seeing what Lethal Length could do for me.

“There didn’t seem to be much product on the wand, but that turned out to be a good thing as the fine bristles created a buildable coat that didn’t ‘blob’ and kept each lash separate. The result was a long feathery finish that opened up the eyes without any lashes sticking together and pointing off in odd directions. The brush allows you to get down to the very base of the lashes, so much so that two coats added sufficient definition to the lash line for me not to need eyeliner. I liked the fact that the lashes stayed soft making an evening recoat easy. The short bristles along one end of the wand help to get into those pesky little hairs on the lower lid. It definitely lives up to its promise to extend, curl and define, although you need more than a single stroke. This is a surprising discovery for me at a great price and something I’ll be buying again.”

Reviewer: VH

6 / 11

Avon True Colour Supreme Length Nourishing Mascara, £10

Buy online

The promise: “The go-to mascara for healthier and longer-looking lashes.”

We give it: 6/10

Review: "When it comes to mascara I’m a promiscuous shopper, having never quite found my perfect match, so I'm always on the look-out for my next potential partner. Avon is not a brand I've tried much of before, so was keen to road test this well priced, Supreme Length Nourishing Mascara in black. The first thing I noticed was the bendy head of the mascara wand, which makes for an easier and more agile charging of the bristles and removal of the excess. Unfortunately it doesn't do much to aid the application process itself, which I found trickier due to the short bristles which mean you need to get the whole wand quite close up to the eyelids to apply. Not a deal breaker but something to get used to and which I find gives more lash separation than length. One thing I do love about this product, however, is the lash rejuvenating complex it boasts, infused with coconut oil, argan oil and vitamin E to help nourish and condition your lashes. It also claims to be kind to sensitive skin around the eyes, though not something I suffer with, it's comforting to know. So far I can vouch for it being clump-free and smudge-proof though I do find the former often comes with age so it remains to be seen if it will stand the test of time. All in all, a good option if you're looking for lash definition, a kind-to-yourself formula at a good price, but not one I'd leave my current partner for!"

Reviewer: GB

7 / 11

Maybelline The Falsies Push Up Angel Mascara, £5.99

Buy online

The promise: “Maybelline’s FIRST ever precision brush proves that the best things come in small packages, as this mascara will be the key to creating gorgeously fluttery lashes for bigger angelic looking eyes, without the hassle of false lashes!”

We give it: 7/10

Review: “Claiming to give your lashes wings and widen eyes, this new small wanded mascara from Maybelline delivered surprisingly big results. Equipped with a precision brush for greater detail and targeted application, it worked a dream for defining, enhancing and lengthening my lower lashes - leaving none of the telltale marks underneath that many a bigger brush has been guilty of in the past. As for its claim to forgo the need for false lashes, I’m not overly convinced. Upper lashes were left fluttery yes, but the results lacked the curve and lift that a pair of falsies provide. That being said though, its pinpoint accuracy has proven a perfect match with my lower lash line, so I’ll definitely keep using it in this regard.”

Reviewer: AM

8 / 11

Barry M Feature Length Mascara, £4.99

Buy online

The promise: “Delivers stunning film-star lashes."

We give it: 8/10

Review: “This mascara is proof that you don’t need to spend £30 on a mascara to get fabulous results. With a super slimline, plastic wand, the brush gets right to the roots and seems to coat every lash from root to tip. I would say it is lengthening rather than volumising and one coat gives a quite natural finish while two gives a more va-va voom flutter. My one hesitation is that applying more than one coat can get slightly tricky - things start to go a bit clumpy, but I actually like a bit of a messy imperfect lash so that doesn’t bother me. Colour wise it is very black (not grey-black as some mascaras are), and it doesn’t end up halfway down your face like some more expensive mascaras do. I wore it for a run in the cold February weather yesterday and then went to a kids’ party afterwards with it all still intact. For the price of a sandwich I really don’t think you can do any better."

Reviewer: ST

9 / 11

GOSH Lash Sculpting Fibre Mascara, £9.99

Buy online

The promise: “Builds visible and measurable longer and fuller lashes.”

We give it: 8/10

Review: “GOSH has a mascara called Boombastic which is quite possibly my favourite mascara of all time - so this one had a lot to live up to. Though the wand is a different shape to one I’d usually pick (I prefer thick, straight, plastic combs) with its tapered end and fine width, I really liked the way this mascara applied - it pulled well through the lashes, really coating them and adding both volume and length. However, having used fibre mascaras before, I am a little confused; GOSH says this contains flower-shaped fibres, up to 2mm in length to help sculpt lashes and fill them out, but I didn’t see any fibres when I applied it, either on my eyes or on the brush. That said, it didn’t impact the results - my lashes looked longer, slightly thicker, and well defined, and weren’t left feeling brittle despite me using a few coats. Fibres or no fibres, I’d buy it again.”

Reviewer: JJ

10 / 11

Bourjois Volume Reveal Mascara, £9.99

Buy online

The promise: "This specially designed brush catches even the shortest of lashes for lash by lash volume without clumps."

We give it: 7/10

Review: "For £9.99 this is probably at the top end of the budget spectrum so you’d expect pretty good results. Designed to improve the volume of your lashes this mascara does what it says on the tin - it reveals lashes you didn’t even know existed. With the name, ‘Radiant Black’ it’s an intense black which I love and after just one coat I felt I had double the amout of lashes, plus the wand is more comb-like than bristled so it’s brilliant at working to separate the lashes. My only personal downfall is that I prefer brushes that are quite bristley, as I like a lot of product to really plump up my lashes, so I wouldn’t be using this one for an evening look - bring back some clumps!"

Reviewer: SM

11 / 11

No7 Intense Volume Mascara, £9.95

Buy online

The promise: “No7 Intense Volume Mascara provides fantastic volume with perfect separation for lusher lashes.”

We give it: 6/10

Review: “When it comes budget makeup and skincare, No7 is with a doubt one of the leaders of the pack. I’m a huge fan of their foundations, moisturisers and lipsticks however, how did this root-boosting mascara measure up? Unfortunately, not as well as I’d hoped. While great for separation, it fell a bit flat for me in terms of providing increased thickness and greater fullness. The fact that it didn’t flake or crumble throughout the day definitely worked in its favour, but it lacked the requisite amount of ‘oomph’ factor that I look for in a volumising mascara.

Reviewer: AM
