10 celebrity long hairstyles that we can’t wait to try

Whether you’re growing out your hair, looking for a range of easy updos or on the hunt for some on-trend hair colour ideas, look no further than our pick of the best celebrity long hair styles around for ample inspiration.

From waves, to curly hair to ombre and balayage, fringes to braids to how to wear your parting, we asked the experts for their top tips for recreating the hairstyles at home.

The one thing that all our experts agree on? Preparation, maintenance and the right hair products are paramount, so click through our gallery to discover some great long hair ideas and what tools you need to make sure your locks look their best from root to tip.

 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Nick Higgins >>>

2 / 11

Olivia Palermo

Sleek and stylish, Olivia Palermo expertly demonstrates how tousled locks can look polished, without looking overly styled.

A frequenter of Neville Hair & Beauty in Belgravia, we asked Director Stephen Low how we too can achieve that dose of natural movement that looks glossy, healthy and effortless. “The new cool Chelsea Hair still has that wave but instead of being such a stiff wave, this is far more natural. These locks tumble in a carefree fashion just like the carefree Chelsea Set! They've ditched the rollers and now these 'in the know' girls use the correct tools. Tongs are the tools of the trade that produce softer, more organic waves.”

“The Chelsea Set has updated their styling products too. The 'holding spray' that leaves hair stiff has been ditched and replaced by Bumble and Bumble Surf Spray, £21.50 to give a natural hold.”


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Nick Higgins >>>

3 / 11

Kim Kardashian

Old school Hollywood fit for the modern day, Kim Kardashian’s elegant waves make for the perfect vintage hairstyle in our opinion. Matched with a deep side parting and a softness to prevent it from looking too stiff, the secret to keeping long hair healthy in the long-term lies in the prep. “Make sure you are using the right shampoo and conditioner, as they can be drying if they contain too much detergent, or if they are not conditioning enough,” says hair stylist Paul Percival. “You should always think of your hair like skin. If you have dry skin you spend money on a good quality moisturiser, and the same applies to hair.”

“The Percy & Reed Totally TLC Hydrating Mask, £20 comes with a bonnet which heats up the head and allows the mask to penetrate further into the hair shaft, giving you a better result,” he recommends. 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Nick Higgins >>>

4 / 11

Beyoncé

Whether it’s ringlets, volume or show-stopping waves, Beyoncé never puts a foot wrong when it comes to making an impact on stage. Always fearless when it comes to her hair, what’s the best way to make curls look their best? “Consider the 5 Cs: Constant Care Creates Controlled Curls,” says hair stylist Errol Douglas MBE. “Twice monthly treatments with potent products are essential and create a consistency in the overall care of the hair. Moroccanoil Hydrating Mask, £24.75 helps to replenish lost nutrients and gives dry hair a boost,” he recommends.

Want the option to go straight? “Don’t blast dry,” he warns. “We say, ‘Go Low Low’ - use a low heat and low speed, it’s the best way to work with a curl to achieve definition and the best route to a frizz-free finish on a sleek straight look.”


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Nick Higgins >>>

5 / 11

Selena Gomez

Taking her cue from fellow fringed beauty Suki Waterhouse, Selena took to her Instagram feed to debut her amazing new bangs that have got us ready to pick up our phones to book in with our hairdressers. “Bangs are the easiest way to reinvent long hair. Today's most seductive, elegant fringe frames the eyes and blends with the rest of the hair,” says Neville Hair and Beauty Director Stephen Low.

How should you decide what type to go for? “When you have finer hair, getting fringes can create instant volume, simply because the front section of the hair appears thicker. For thick, even coarse or wavy hair, opt for a longer fringe that can easily be pushed to the side - having a little movement to hair actually works with the finish,” recommends Sassoon UK Creative Director Bruce Masefield. “A side-fringe is the easiest to maintain and suits most people. Fuller fringes are flattering on girls with big eyes and an oval-shaped face,” he adds.

6 / 11

Alexa Chung

Growing out your hair? Then look no further than Alexa Chung for the perfect hair inspiration. “We’re seeing long choppy tresses with blunt ends,” says hair stylist and Pureology Ambassador George Northwood. “Redken Quick Tease, £15.80 gives the hair great texture and plenty of body to achieve that undone finish.”

“It’s key to keep the hair in good condition though,” he adds. “Pureology Strength Cure, nourishes long lengths and prevents breakage, allowing the hair to keep on growing! Fabulous Lengths, £14 is a must-have product from the range which strengthens the hair fibre and gives softness.”


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Nick Higgins >>>

7 / 11

Jared Leto

Regardless of whether you’re a man or a woman, we just can’t get enough of the Oscar winner’s glossy ombre hair worthy of a Rockstar.

“The great thing about ombre is that is can be done on all types of hair and colours,” says Andrew John, Colour Director at Percy & Reed. “With darker brunettes the ombre stands out more, while on naturally lighter blondes ombre will be more of a subtle blend.”

“For brunettes I would always recommend richer tones, while for blondes you can go for mink violet tones. Ombre is a low maintenance colour but I would always recommend a consultation with your stylists and using a rich mask at home like our Percy & Reed TLC Hydrating Mask, £20 to keep colour vibrant."


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Nick Higgins >>>

8 / 11

Nicole Scherzinger

Braids, no matter how big or small, are a quick and easy way to update your long hairstyle of choice. Follow Nicole Scherzinger’s lead and opt for a fishtail plait (find out how to do one here), or opt for a crown of festival hair like we did at V Festival courtesy of Mark Hill.

Spritz hair with Bedazzled Anti-Humidity Shine Spray, £5.99 and start braiding back and to one side from your front hairline. As you braid, ‘drop a section of hair to fall loosely down and secure the ends using a silicone band. Enhance waves using a Mark Hill Rock Chick Wave Wand, £29.99. Finally smooth a few drops of MiracOILicious Argan Oil, £9.99 through lengths to protect, define and add shine. 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Nick Higgins >>>

9 / 11

Cheryl Cole

Hair updos for long hair can be a tricky art to master, however Cheryl Cole nailed it with this multi-dimensional bun with just the right amount of polish to make it red carpet appropriate.

“The first rule for putting hair up is preparation. You need to get some guts into the hair,” says the team at Percy & Reed. “A great way to do this is by using a few sprays of No Fuss Fabulousness Dry Shampoo, £12, which will clean away any excess oils and give your style a longer life.”

“For that Bardot volume, use the BaByliss Root Boost, £30,” they add. “It’s a very quick and easy tool to use. Take 1 inch sections through the crown area of your hair and use the small crimper to crimp the base of each section, spraying each section with Percy & Reed Reassuringly Firm Session Hairspray, £12 before crimping. Remember to leave a section of uncrimped hair to hide the crimping effect in the style.”

10 / 11

Kate Middleton

Not only is Kate Middleton our future Queen in waiting, but she’s also Queen of the glossiest hair we’ve ever seen too. “To achieve long lustrous locks aka the Duchess of Cambridge and the Chelsea set, you need to treat your hair like it were Royalty,” recommends top session stylist Stephen Low. “There is no instantaneous method for growing hair, but conscientious application of sound hair strengthening practices will help your locks achieve their longest potential.”

The secret lies in cleverly placed subtle layers, “We've all had cookie-cutter layers - the kind that are long in the back and angle straight down from the jaw in the front,” says Stephen. “Here's what sets celebrities' haircuts apart: they never have those kinds of layers. Theirs are 007 discreet, stealthily adding curves.”

His top haircare tips? “Use a satin pillowcase to avoid nightly snarls. Add some almond oil on the tips of your hair so your hair oozes health and happiness. Don't brush hair too frequently; doing so risks broken strands and stripping protective oils.”

11 / 11

Sienna Miller

Boho hair fit for a ball, one of our favourite hair heroes shows that updos needn’t look perfect to fit in with a black tie theme.

“The first rule for putting hair up is preparation. You need to get some guts into the hair,” says the team at Percy & Reed. “A great way to do this is by using a few sprays of No Fuss Fabulousness Dry Shampoo, £12, which will clean away any excess oils and give your style a longer life.”

Use your fingers instead of brush to scrape hair back for a more lived-in feel. Twist a plait into a bun, then pin and spritz with hairspray to finish.
