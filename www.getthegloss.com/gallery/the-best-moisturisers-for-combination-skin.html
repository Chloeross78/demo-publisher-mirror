The best moisturisers for combination skin

  • March 5th 2017
  • / Ayesha Muttucumaru 
  • / 2 comments

1 / 10

The best moisturisers for combination skin

Caring for combination skin can cause quite the conundrum in the skincare stakes - especially when it comes to picking the perfect daily moisturiser. Dry in some areas, oilier in others, the variations differ from person to person. However, whether you’re on the slicker or thirstier ends of the skincare spectrum, we’ve tried a variety of different moisturisers to compile a comprehensive edit from which to choose from. From gels to lotions, high street to high end, we hope you find your perfect formula here.

Follow Ayesha on Twitter and Instagram.

2 / 10

Clarins Hydra-Matte Lotion

Oily T-zone? Then this cream’s for you. With an impressive ability to leave skin matte, not flat, a combination of plant-based ingredients including alpine willow herb and white tea helps to de-slick shine-prone areas while also leaving skin feeling refreshed and ready for the day ahead.

£27. Buy online.

3 / 10

La Roche-Posay Hydraphase UV Intense Legere SPF 20

For those whose skin veers towards the drier end of the skincare spectrum, this parched complexion quencher provides the perfect solution. Equipped with Thermal Spring Water (a brand signature), glycerin and sodium hyaluronate to hydrate and broad spectrum UVA and UVB sun filters to protect, it leaves skin glowing.

£17. Buy online.

4 / 10

Nivea Daily Essentials Light Moisturising Cream for normal to combination skin SPF15

Hydrating without feeling heavy on skin, this budget beauty buy delivers high quality for a refreshingly high street price. Leaving complexions feeling noticeably more supple and smooth, it balances moisture with shine-control beautifully.

£4.10. Buy online.

5 / 10

Origins GinZing Energy Boosting Moisturiser

Light in texture, this oil-free moisturiser’s gel-like and fast-absorbing consistency makes it an ideal base for makeup. With a lovely scent, its energising combination of panax ginseng and coffee bean works as an effective fatigue-fighter - a hugely popular double act that resulted it being out of stock for a short while. But loyal fans needn’t worry - it’s now back.

£24. Buy online. 

6 / 10

Sarah Chapman Skinesis Dynamic Defence SPF 15

Offering impressive anti-ageing prowess, this moisturiser is as much a treat as it is a longer-term treatment. Enriched with collagen and elastin boosting oil soluble vitamin A, antioxidants, shea butter, mango butter and other skin goodies to help even tone and texture, it provides a valuable complexion pick-me-up with each daily pump.

£49. Buy online. 

7 / 10

Clinique Pep-start Hydrorush Moisturizer SPF 20

As lovely to use as it is to look at, this sunny-looking moisturiser is multisensory in its appeal. Providing both style and substance, it adopts a multi-faceted approach towards prevention and protection courtesy of a combination of broad spectrum sunscreens, ingredients such as barley and wheat germ extract to strengthen skin barrier, hyaluronic acid to hydrate, peptides to plump and anti-irritants to keep skin feeling comfortable and conditioned from morning until evening.

£22.05. Buy online. 

8 / 10

Kiehl’s Ultra Facial Moisturiser

Formulated with squalane (a botanical lipid derived mainly from olive fruit trees) to provide natural-feeling moisturisation and vitamins A and E, this hydrator provides both short-term softenss and long-lasting suppleness. Noticeably more fluid in its texture, its spreads like a dream and a little goes much further than expected.

From £13.95. Buy online.

9 / 10

Pai Perfect Balance Geranium & Thistle Rebalancing Day Cream

A makeup artist favourite, the non-greasy and nourishing nature of this day cream gives it a distinct appeal. With essential fatty acids from thistle and borage oils to calm skin, a balmy scent to calm senses and geranium oil to help rebalance skin’s sebum production, its non-cloying texture is ideal for those looking for fast hydration, but want to forego the oily finish.

£34. Buy online.

10 / 10

Paula’s Choice Skin Balancing Invisible Finish Moisture Gel

Invisible by name, invisible by nature, this shine and grease-free pick is akin to the silkiest of skin slips. We loved how quickly it absorbed and its traceless finish - a great combo for those who's combo skin veers on the oilier end of the spectrum.

£22.50. Buy online.
