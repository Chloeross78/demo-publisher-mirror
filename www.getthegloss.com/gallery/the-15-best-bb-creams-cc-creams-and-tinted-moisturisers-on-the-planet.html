15 best BB creams, CC creams and tinted moisturisers on the planet

  • May 17th 2016
  • / Ayesha Muttucumaru , Anna Hunter

The 15 best BB creams, CC creams and tinted moisturisers on the planet

Looking for an alternative to foundation but don’t know where to start? A BB cream, CC cream or tinted moisturiser could prove to be your perfect match.

There’s an array of cover up alternatives to choose from, but which ones are the best of the bunch? Here at GTG HQ, we’ve been testing the full repertoire of more wearable daily tints to bring you our definitive guide for any skin type and skin tone. Whether you’re looking to address uneven skin tone, feign a glow or are eager to try the future in customisable makeup, here are the best BB creams, CC creams and tinted moisturisers on the planet that really deliver on their promises.

Follow us @getthegloss, Anna @AnnaMaryHunter and Ayesha @Ayesha_Muttu.  


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

2 / 16

Bobbi Brown BB Cream

Shade range: 9 shades from ‘Extra Light’ to ‘Rich’ covering light to deep skin tones

Skin types: All

Coverage: Sheer, buildable

SPF: SPF 35

GTG verdict: Providing the perfect amounts of coverage and skincare savvy to improve skin texture and appearance both in the short and long-term, this BB cream is one of the best for creating the most flawless of canvases when you’re short on time. Blendable, buildable and easy to apply with fingers or a brush (we preferred the former), it’s the type of makeup multitasker capable of helping you make significant strides when it comes to streamlining your beauty regime.

Buy online. £29.50. 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

3 / 16

NARS Pure Radiant Tinted Moisturizer

Shade range: 11 shades available ranging from fair (‘Finland’s’ your match) to deep (think ‘Seychelles’ and ‘Polynesia’)

Skin types: Dry and combination skin types will benefit most

Coverage: Sheer

SPF: SPF 30/PA+++

GTG verdict: For beautifully glowing skin, look no further than this oil-free tinted moisturiser from NARS. Illuminating without looking greasy, it provides seamless radiant coverage while also keeping skin wonderfully hydrated underneath too - a daily complexion pick-me-up in a bottle.

Buy online. £29.50

 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

4 / 16

Lancôme Miracle Cushion

Shade range: 8 shades - from the lightest to the darkest of skin tones

Skin types: All

Coverage: Sheer to high - it’s surprisingly easy to find your finish of choice and navigate your way along the coverage spectrum

SPF: SPF 23/PA++

GTG verdict: Not quite a BB cream, not quite a foundation, this makeup hybrid sits comfortably between both bases to provide an innovative option in the cover up market. A unique beauty buy, its roots stem from Asia offering a level of coverage that can be custom-blended using its sponge cushion. Simply press and swipe the light, fluid formula using the pad applicator and pop into your handbag for easy touch-ups on-the-go too.

Buy online. £19.50. 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

5 / 16

Burberry Fresh Glow BB Cream

Shade range: 3 shades - fair, medium and dark which are best suited for light and light olive skin tones

Skin types: All

Coverage: Sheer, natural-looking - perfect for those looking for a dose of luminosity

SPF: SPF 20

GTG verdict: This super lightweight beauty balm glides effortlessly onto skin to provide luminous coverage that doesn’t clog pores. Glow-enhancing and hydrating, it provides just the right amount of coverage to even skin tone without weighing it down to enhance rather than mask for a radiant and natural finish.

Buy online. £30. 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

6 / 16

Laura Mercier Tinted Moisturiser

Shade range: Impressive - 15 shades ranging from ‘Porcelain’ to ‘Mocha’

Skin types: All

Coverage: Sheer, buildable

SPF: SPF 20

GTG verdict: Lightweight and supremely hydrating, it’s no wonder that this tinted moisturiser has achieved cult status. With a bevy of skin conditioning goodies to boost your complexion from the inside out, we were particularly impressed by its silky texture and finish - think bare skin, just better.

Buy online. £34. 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

7 / 16

Cover FX Custom Cover Drops

Shade range: A mind-blowing 24. TWENTY FOUR

Skin types: All

Coverage: Pretty much any - that’s the beauty of this product. If you have oily skin, mix with a mattifying moisturiser. Dry skin? Pop it into your favourite rich moisturiser. The possibilities are endless...

SPF: The level of added protection depends on the product you mix it with...

GTG verdict: Fancy a tinted moisturiser one day, a more intense level of coverage the next? You can have your pick of both with this versatile bottle of pigment drops in your makeup bag. Simply add one or four drops (depending on the finish you’re after) to your favourite moisturiser, serum, oil or primer and merge your skincare with some inventive makeup moxy for a weightless, bespoke finish.

£36. Buy online. 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

8 / 16

Bobbi Brown Tinted Moisturiser

Shade range: 8 shades available suiting light to medium dark skin tones

Skin types: Dry and combination skin types will love this particular tinted moisturiser, suiting teen to mature skin types to a T

Coverage: Sheer, natural

SPF: SPF 15

GTG verdict: As far as bases that provide more bang for your buck, this tinted moisturiser is one of the best - only a small amount is needed for its effects to start being noticed. It moisturises, protects and provides smooth even coverage in one fell swoop, leaving skin smoother, softer and more even to offer a versatile alternative to your wardrobe of cover ups.   

Buy online. £29.50. 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

9 / 16

Guerlain Super Aqua Serum BB Hydra+

Shade range: Two. Light or Medium. That’s your lot.

Skin types: Dry to normal. Sensitive skins possibly steer clear due to the amount of fragrance in the formula. 

Coverage: Light

SPF: 25

GTG verdict: If you like your BB creams light as air with a side of potent skincare power, this wordy Guerlain option will be right up your rue. Offering more of a flattering veil of colour than actual coverage, it contains Guerlain’s ‘aquacomplex’, which purifies cellular water to boost circulation and moisture levels. On a hydration level, it’s a winner, but if you’re skin is darker than mid-olive, you’ll struggle with this one. Otherwise, subtle and buildable.

Buy online, £39.60 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

10 / 16

Chanel CC Cream

Shade range: Four. Superb if you’re very pale but doesn’t cater for dark skintones.

Skin types: Dry to normal

Coverage: Light

SPF:50

GTG verdict: Recently reformulated, it’s only right that CC leads the CC brigade it terms of stylishly sheer coverage and supreme product intelligence. It deems itself as more skincare than makeup, and while the pore and redness diminishment is welcome, the high SPF, plumping hyaluronic acid and protective, pollution fighting antioxidants put it in the superhero CC category. Skin is smooth, lovely and just ‘better’ after application, minus the faff of actual foundation. Pricey, but quite literally covers a lot of bases.

Buy online, £44 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

11 / 16

Bareminerals Complexion Rescue

Shade range: 10, from pale Vanilla to deep Sienna. There should be something here for everyone.

Skin types: Very dry to normal skin

Coverage: Very light

SPF: 30 PA+++

GTG verdict: Healthy looking, radiant, dewy...all ‘great skin’ buzzwords, and all attainable via this unique hydrating yet very lightweight CC come BB come tinted moisturiser. Just so we’re clear, it’s nowhere near a foundation, and it won’t conceal blemishes or lumps and bumps, but it will give especially dry skin a glow, not to mention leave it comfortable, smooth, soft and well protected from the sun. The fragrance free formula is a plus.

Buy online, £26 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

12 / 16

MAC Prep+Prime Beauty Balm

Shade range: 12. Impressive indeed for a BB.

Skin types: Normal to oily and combination

Coverage: Medium

SPF: 35 PA+++

GTG verdict: If you’re after a BB for oily skin, this could be the ‘one’. BBs can often slip quite a bit if you’re prone to a bit of midday grease, but this has an almost matte finish. It evens out skintone very well, is nicely hydrating (although drier skins will require a serum or moisturiser underneath) and offers good, reliable sun protection, plus a dollop of youth-preserving antioxidants.

Buy online, £25 

 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

13 / 16

Clinique Moisture Surge CC Cream

Shade range: 9, from very pale to deep. Speaking as someone often too fair for this tinted business, the shade range is bang on (Ayesha finds it fitting too).

Skin types: Very dry to normal

Coverage: Medium

SPF: 30

GTG verdict: Far more than just a colour corrector, this Clinique marvel is possibly my favourite CC created. Firstly, it’s really moisturising. It feels cool and creamy to apply, and leaves skin beaming, bouncy and all delicious feeling. It smooths on in a non-streaky way (not a given in this product sector), disguises shadows, brings down redness and blends beautifully with foundation if you have one that you’d like to inject a bit of moisture and oomph into. Sun protection wise it’s safe and sound. Smart stuff.

Buy online, £28

 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

14 / 16

Yves Saint Laurent Top Secrets All in One BB Cream

Shade range: Not a lot of good if you’re dark skinned. Just two colour options- Light and Medium. We have heard tell of a Dark shade but can’t find it offered anywhere...comment below if you find it!

Skin types: Dry to combination

Coverage: Medium

SPF: 25

GTG verdict: This BB lists ‘priming’ as one of its many talents, and while we’ve no doubt that it creates the ideal canvas for makeup, this is one of the actually fairly rare protective, corrective creams that behaves like a foundation in terms of finish and coverage. It’s light, you can apply more where you need without it caking and with a touch of powder it won’t desert you by lunchtime. Hydration wise it smooths over dry patches nicely, and while you might wish to top up the SPF, it’s a good start. The shade options fall seriously short, however.

Buy online, £39.50 

 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

15 / 16

L’Oréal Paris Skin Perfection BB 5 in 1 Instant Blemish Balm

Shade range: Another measly offering- just Fair and Medium

Skin types: Oily to combination and normal

Coverage: Light

SPF: 25

GTG verdict: The best of the high street haul, this BB cream smooths on evenly, blurs bumps and blotches without feeling too ‘slick’ and most noticeably minimises the appearance of pores without suffocating the skin. Those with dry skins will likely need to prep skin with a moisturiser or serum, but this is a good BB option if you have oily skin; it should offer up just enough moisture. The SPF rating is higher than that of your average non-premium base, so adding concealer or a touch of foundation where you need it could make it the ideal all rounder. There’s only one caveat; you need to be pale to only slightly suntanned. Sigh.

Buy online, £12.99 

 


NEXT GALLERY: My Week in Food: Mario Kaspers, co-founder of E-Pulsive >>>

16 / 16

Chantecaille Just Skin Tinted Moisturizer

Shade range: 7 shades, but all on the light to medium spectrum.

Skin types: Normal to dry

Coverage: Light

SPF: 15

GTG verdict: Sheer and perfecting without being in any way heavy or obvious, this is an undeniably spendy tinted moisturizer, but we’ve had our tube for quite some time now and it's seemingly never ending. It has to be said that it’s strong point isn’t the SPF (it’s advisable to wear something higher), but the brightening, barely there finish looks suitably expensive. As far as coverage goes, it’s as fresh as it gets, and adding concealer where you need it will convince onlookers that you simply woke up with a rested glow. Apparently it’s so imperceptible, men can wear it. Watch that tube like a hawk, you don’t want this going walkies.

Buy online, £58
