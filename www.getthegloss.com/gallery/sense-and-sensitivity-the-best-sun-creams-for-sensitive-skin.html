The best sun creams for sensitive skin

Sun cream has always been a dilemma for me and my lifelong battle with sensitive skin, with holidays that are filled with rashes and reactions instead of simply sun, sea, sand and sangria. Those of us who are sensitive need to shop carefully for our sun protection; whether it's avoiding fragrance, irritating preservatives or chemical filters, it's important to know exactly what you're buying. As for me, I tend to avoid all of the above - fragrance is one of the most common allergens, preservatives such as MI can hurt even the stronger of skin and chemical filters aren't recommended for the fragile. (See my ultimate guide to SPF ingredients for sensitive skin here.)

Sun cream should be no different to your skincare - don't simply buy what's on offer, look closer at the label and know what you'll be putting on your face and body. Luckily, I've done the research for you; click through the gallery for my top sun screens for sensitive skin and enjoy the summer while it lasts.

Ultrasun Extreme SPF 50+ for ultra sensitive skin

This is the discovery that changed my holidays forever, and Ultrasun is my number one go-to for SPF. I use the Ultrasun Family 30 Super Sensitive for once-a-day application that doesn't leave you looking white or red, or this SPF 50+ for extra strength without the pain of reactions. What's more, the range is better than ever, with recent launches including a full Anti-Pigmentation range, a hand and eye cream, and beautiful tinted formulas for face and body.

Ultrasun is one of the very few SPFs I use that doesn't contain mineral-only filters (it uses a combination); however, its credentials are in its preservative- and fragrance-free formula. As MD Abi Cleeves explains, "Consumers have been made aware of certain preservatives which are causing dramatic allergic reactions leaving some people with painful swollen skin; most beauty products will contain preservatives however certain types such as Methylisothiazolinone (MI) which is also used to preserve paint has been proven to cause contact dermatitis leaving skin red, sore and swollen after being applied directly to the skin. All Ultrasun products do not contain nor have ever contained MI as it is of the utmost importance to Ultrasun that our high quality, paraben-free products remain suitable for all sensitive skin types while also providing the very best in sun protection.”

From £19 for 150ml, Buy online

It contains only mineral SPF filters and no parabens or fragrance - I never go on holiday without it.

Dermaceutic Sun Ceutic 50 Anti Aging Sun Protection SPF50

This brand is new to me, but all I needed to know I wanted it on my face was the four key ingredients listed on the front of the packaging: zinc oxide, titanium dioxide, hyaluronic acid and aloe vera. The dream team for sensitive skin - mineral or 'physical' filters, a hydration booster and nature's most calming anti-inflammatory ingredient, preventing dehydration, irritation and ageing in one. In a sleek pump bottle, the cream is velvety soft without being heavy, and though white at first it's transparent once rubbed in. Skin feels truly moisturised and soothed at the same time; heaven.

Clinique City Block Sheer SPF25

I love, love, love this product. A sheer, tinted, fragrance-free sun cream with no chemical filters, it ticks all the boxes for everyday wear which is why I wear it year-round come rain or shine; we need sun protection whatever the weather after all. Available in SPF40 too, it offers not so much coverage but more a gentle glow that leaves your skin looking radiant while leaving you safe in the knowledge it's preventing premature ageing and sun damage. Lightweight and oil free, it glides on like a moisturiser but can be used as a primer or instead of your BB cream. Flawless.

As of May 2016, Clinique has also launched two new mineral filter-only sun screens for face (SPF50) and body (SPF30), again both fragrance-free and ideal for sensitive skin. Lightweight and non-greasy, they make sun protection a breeze.

Colorescience Sunforgettable Broad Spectrum SPF30 Loose Mineral Sunscreen

I'm not a fan of SPF in makeup (it gives a false sense of protection, in my opinion, as you rarely use enough) but this product is something special. A loose, finely milled mineral powder with a built-in brush, it swirls onto the skin to leave a beautifully flawless coverage that would impress as a base alone; add to that the SPF 30 due to the titanium dioxide and zinc oxide filters and you've got a winner. (N.B. To be safe, I would still use a cream SPF as my moisturiser underneath for peace of mind.)

I tried the Matte version in Medium, and it was a perfect match for my fair skin - and though I tend to go for more of a dewy finish with my makeup, I wasn't disappointed. It left my skin radiant but protected (it's very water resistant, check out this impressive video). Best of all, it makes the reapplication of sun protection for the face a doddle - because who honestly reapplies any once their makeup is on? Simply top up with this every couple of hours and you're good to go. It's pricey, but refills are cheaper and for anyone who wants an everyday SPF that won't clog or feel heavy, this is it.

Bjobj Baby Solar Fluid Cream SPF50

Who has the most sensitive skin? *Puts hand up* Well, apart from me: babies. I find it fascinating that we absolutely refuse to put fragrance or harmful ingredients on children yet are willing to slather ourselves in them (and products are marketed as such) but the good thing is, there are no rules against us adults stealing their products and this lotion is one worthy of thieving.

An organic sun cream (certified by Italy's organic certification body, ICEA), it's labelled as a kids' product but with factor 50 sun protection created by mineral filters it ticks all of my boxes. It contains no colourings, perfumes, chemical filters or parabens, and is full of deliciously nourishing ingredients such as argan oil, mallow extract and vitamin E. It goes on like any high street SPF - light, fluid and easy to rub in. Buy it for you, for your kids, whoever - if you're into your organics, just buy it, full stop.

Neal's Yard Remedies Lemongrass Moisturising Sun Lotion

If you like your skincare to be organic, this new sun cream from Neal's Yard Remedies is the one to beat. Their 'If in doubt, leave it out' ingredients principle and stamp of Soil Association Certified approval means the inci list makes for happy reading - it's rich in skin-softening shea butter, as well as oils packed with fatty acids such as karanja oil, baobab oil and sunflower oil. Available in SPF 15-50, the mineral UV filters zinc oxide and titanium dioxide provide protection from the sun while the beautiful lemongrass essential oil is not only anti-bacterial but insect-repellent, too. Of all the creams tested, this has the most luxurious feel (something we fragile folk rarely get to experience, amIright?) - I'd happily use it as a body lotion, sun or no sun.

Garnier Ambre Solaire Sensitive Advanced 50+ Lotion

My main issue with sensitive-friendly sun screens is that you have to really shop around for them - until recently there’s been no chance of waiting until you’re at the airport and picking something suitable up in Boots. Garner Ambre Solaire used to be one of the SPFs I’d react to (fragrance the most likely culprit) but in 2016 they launched their Sensitive Advanced formulas. Still containing chemical sunscreens, albeit a relatively skin-safe one called Mexoryl SX (read more about it here) and octocrylene, it’s perfume-free, paraben-free, broad spectrum and a great budget option if you can tolerate some of the less irritating chemical sun screens. It’s the one I’d reach for if I found myself in the airport without one of my favourite mineral SPFs.
