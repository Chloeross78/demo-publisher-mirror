The Gloss Report: False eyelashes

False eyelashes may be a fun way to add a sprinkling of glamour to your party ensemble but sourcing that perfect pair can be tricky. Too big and you enter ‘drag’ territory, too small and you wonder why you bothered. A winning pair of false lashes should involve fuss free application and stay put from cocktails to cab. The Gloss team put the best to the test…

 


NEXT GALLERY: Christmas Gift Ideas: the best buys for a fashionista >>>

2 / 7

Laura Mercier Corner Faux Lashes

Price: £14.50

Buy online

Feedback: "I’ve been rather wary of false lashes since a string of incidents involving loosing a half a set mid night-out and with it half my eye makeup – never a good look. However, with party season approaching I was excited to give them another go and was immediately drawn to this natural looking pair of corner lashes from Laura Mercier. I applied them using my fail-safe technique of a pair of tweezers and a handy cotton bud and found them pretty easy to apply. Once in place the eyelashes gave a lovely subtle lift to the corner of my eyes and provided a great partner to winged eyeliner. Plus, most importantly, they stayed in place all night long."

Reviewer: EB

Score: 9/10

3 / 7

Illamasqua False Eye Lashes No.16

Price: £12

Buy online

Feedback: "As a former fake eyelash addict, it’s safe to say that I’ve experienced every type of falsie faux pas going (and sadly have the photos to show for it). So considering my rather hairy (sorry) past, how did this pair fare in comparison to the rest? Pretty well actually.

These eyelashes are all about high drama and impact, certainly more for night than for day. Designed to elongate peepers for an almost cat-eye effect, they’re particularly good for length and definition as opposed to volume and curl.

However, due to their shape, the length and the consistency of the glue, I found them a little fiddly to put on and ended up with quite a lot post-application mess. In that sense, I would say they’re a better fit for those who are a bit more used to wearing false eyelashes. However, once cleaned up and secured, I really liked the overall effect. Fuller and more eye-catching, I can feel my addiction slowly coming back, especially in the lead up to Saturday night."

Reviewer: AM

Score: 7/10

4 / 7

Mac False Eyelashes No. 33

Price: £10.50

Buy online 

Feedback: “I love the look of false lashes but they have to feel weightless else I’m back to my mascara in a flash - luckily, these fit the bill. A fluttery strip of lashes which are set in separate lash bundles, these are light, long and feathery without being too ‘TOWIE’ - ideal for that lash extension look without going OTT. They are on a sticky strip which isn’t strong enough to adhere to your lash line, and glue is sold separately - a little inconvenient, as who ever keeps track of those tiny tubes of glue you get elsewhere? However, with some careful glue application I found them easy to put on, didn’t need to trim them and found they made my eyes look bigger and flirtier, without too much drama. Great if you’re looking for a subtle flutter.”

Reviewer: JJ

Score: 7/10

5 / 7

Katy Perry for Eylure in 'Cool Kitty'

Price: £5.95

Buy online 

Feedback: "When I saw that pop princess Katy Perry had teamed up with Eylure to create a set of purse-friendly eyelashes, I was curious to see if the range could help me achieve the wide-eyed smoky look that the singer always manages to pull off. I tried 'Cool Kitty', a beautiful set of lashes perfect for adding a subtle hint of drama to your evening look.

These lashes have manicured tips, which provide a defined frame around the eyes, while still looking quite natural. They add the perfect amount of thickness and length to make your lashes appear really full and fluttery.

They're also very easy to apply and not as messy as false lashes I've tried in the past. Definitely a bonus."

Reviewer: HI

Score: 10/10

6 / 7

Millie Mackintosh Belgravia Lashes

Price: £8.99

Buy online

Feedback: "Always sporting noticeably voluminous lashes of her own, I had high hopes for these ‘Belgravia’ falsies from MIC star Millie Mackintosh - and I’m pleased to admit I wasn’t disappointed at all. In fact, I’d probably go as far to say they were probably the best false eyelashes I’ve yet to try.

The perfect combination of thickness and length, they gave my regular lashes an added boost of natural looking glamour without being overwhelmingly big or too ‘drag’. Also coming complete with a step-by-step guide and a miniature pair of tweezers they were relatively easy to both apply and remove. Not to mention this result was achieved at such a meagre, modest price. With 7 other styles available I’ll definitely be heading back to Chelsea for my false lash fix."

Score: 9/10

Reviewer: KR

7 / 7

Benefit Starlet Lashes

Price: £12

Buy online

Feedback: “These lashes were designed to achieve a doe eyed look- perfect for my small eyes in two ways. First, they did just that- my flutter was very 'come hither' if I do say so myself and the effect was quite retro. If you're going to wear falsies, chances are you want to make at least a bit of an impact, so I enjoyed the throwback movie star effect. Secondly the fact that they didn't extend into the inner corners of my eyes was a blessing as it meant that I didn't have to trim the strip as I normally do (very fiddly). The only downside was that my pair didn't come with lash glue, so I had to search my flat for some crusty adhesive of questionable age. This was not an ideal situation as the glue itself was clearly past it and didn't stick. If you’ve got a decent glue, you're good to go - the lashes were incredibly easy to fit and apply”

Rating: 9/10

Reviewer: AH
